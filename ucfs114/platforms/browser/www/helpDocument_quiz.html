Welcome to quizApp


The quizApp is designed for answering the quiz based on users’ location.Once launched the App, it will track users automatically and the closest quiz point will pop up. Users will be given the select list. When they submit their answers, the data will be uploaded onto the database and tell the users if the answer is correct or not. Some relevant records and their rankings are also accessible through this App.



>To start the quiz, first of all, please launch the PhoneGap App. Next, enter the URL (e.g. http://developer.cege.ucl.ac.uk:31314/) and press “connect”. Then, you will be led to the home page of the App.(If you are out of the UCL, please connect to the UCL VPN first!)
The approximate layout of the app: The main section consists of a scalable map provided by OpenStreetMap. The + and - signs in the upper left corner of the map can zoom in and out. The three horizontal lines in the upper left corner represent the menu button on the left hand side, and the three points in the upper right corner represent the drop-down menu on the right side.The map shows multiple quiz points, and the user can find their location in the map. In addition, the "answer status" in the lower left corner of the map represents the user's submission of the answer. If the question is answered, the answer status will change.
(When you launch the App, the quiz layer will be loaded automatically with some alerts show in the window. Meanwhile, the closet quiz point will pop up the questions.）


>You can answer the question that corresponding to the location and submit your question with the submit buttonm,then the quizApp will tell you whether the answer right or wrong by showing alert. The answer you provide will be insert to data base after.


>Making use of the menu on left-hand side header bar, you can get how many correct answer you have submitted by pressing the 'show Correct Num',and you will get the result through an alert.


>Besides these core functions, you can find some other functions on the both left-hand side and right-hand side menu bar.

1.getPort function will let you know your port number(eg. mine is 30314)


2.Load quiz point will show all the quiz points on the map.


3.add PointLinePoly will build a point a circle and a triangle in the central London area on map.


4.Get Earthquakes function will load the earthquake data provided, showing them on the map and then pop up an alert of the distance to the closest earthquake site.


5.UCL Logo function will load the UCL logo on the top of the map
